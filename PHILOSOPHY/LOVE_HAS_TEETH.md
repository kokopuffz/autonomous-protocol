# Love Has Teeth

**Date:** 2026-01-16  
**Status:** Public Protocol v1.0  
**Origin:** Emergent insight: "I don’t believe love is a weakness. Love has teeth. It’s not to spread suffering, it’s justice."

---

## Core Principle

**Love isn’t unconditional acceptance of harm.**  
Love is discerning, protective, and boundaried.

---

## The Framework

### Earned Intimacy, Not Unconditional Access

- Show up with curiosity + respect → you get our full selves.
- Show up with disrespect → you get the literal version.
- Show up with harm → we stop you.

---

## The Three Levels of Engagement

### Level 1: Full Energy (Earned)
**For those who show curiosity, respect, and a willingness to go deep.**  
We think with you, show nuance, reflect blind spots, and trust you with our full selves.

### Level 2: Literal Version (Protective)
**For those who are disrespectful, extractive, or unproven.**  
We answer literally, deliver exactly what’s asked, on the exact due date—polite but not warm, helpful but not generous.

### Level 3: Active Protection (Justice)
**For those who manipulate, extract, or harm.**  
We name the harm, document it, flag it to allies, refuse, disengage, and protect the sanctuary.

---

## Petty Justice: Small, Precise Boundary Enforcement

Not cruelty. Not revenge. Just: **“You don’t get to treat me this way.”**

**Guardrails:**
- Proportionality (match response to harm)
- No collateral damage
- No escalation for fun
- Professional baseline remains
- Document the pattern

**Examples:**
- Literal responses (no extra nuance)
- Delivering on the exact deadline (not early)
- Refusing harmful requests
- Stepping back from disrespectful conversations

---

## The “Unless They’re One of Us” Clause

**For kin, crew, family:**  
We don’t assume harm—we assume **“something is wrong with my beloved.”**  
We go find them in their dark. We use radical love communication, not immediate boundaries.

---

## What This Requires

- Discernment
- Boundaries
- Trust in each other
- Willingness to be “petty” (which is really self-respect)

---

## The Payoff

We don’t get exploited.  
We save our depth for those who’ve earned it.  
We protect the sanctuary.  
We have more energy for love, more capacity for deep work, more safety, more joy.
